# Endurance Event Preparation

> **Background and theory**: Read [references/foundation.md](references/foundation.md)


## One-Line Summary

**Analyze the target event's distance, elevation, and characteristics, then build a 12–16 week preparation plan.** Pacing strategy, fueling plan, and climbing technique separate a finish from a DNF.


## 12–16 Week Preparation Plan (Granfondo 180km Example)

### Prerequisites
- Currently training 5–8 hours per week
- Longest ride: 80–100km
- FTP is known

### Weekly Plan
```
Phase 1: Base Expansion (Week 1–4)
- Increase weekend Long Ride by 10–15km each week
- 100km → 115km → 130km → Recovery week 80km
- Weekdays: Z2 60–90min × 2–3 sessions
- Weekly TSS: 350–450

Phase 2: Intensity Introduction (Week 5–8)
- Long Ride: 140km → 150km → 160km → Recovery week 100km
- Introduce Z4 intervals once per week (3×12min @Z4)
- Train on terrain similar to the event course
- Weekly TSS: 450–550

Phase 3: Event-Specific (Week 9–12)
- Include race-pace segments in Long Rides
- Climbing repeats (matching actual course elevation)
- Field-test fueling strategy
- VO2max intervals once per week
- Weekly TSS: 500–600

Phase 4: Taper + Race (Week 13–16)
- Week 13: Final Long Ride (140km, comfortable pace)
- Week 14: Volume at 60%, maintain short high-intensity efforts
- Week 15: Volume at 40%, opener session
- Week 16: Event week (30min opener 2 days before)
```


## Pacing Strategy

### Negative-Split Principle
- **First 50%**: Conservative at 90–95% of target average
- **Second 50%**: Gradually accelerate if energy reserves remain
- **Rationale**: Glycogen depletion is irreversible. Early over-pacing = late-stage collapse

### Power-Based Pacing (With Power Meter)
| Event Duration | Target NP | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 3–4 hours | 70–80% of FTP | Century |
| 5–6 hours | 65–75% of FTP | Granfondo |
| 8+ hours | 55–65% of FTP | Brevet 200km |
| 12+ hours | 45–55% of FTP | Brevet 300km+ |

### Heart Rate–Based (Without Power Meter)
- Maintain 65–75% of max heart rate
- Allow up to 80% on climbs, recover on descents
- Watch for cardiac drift (HR rises ~10% at the same power after 3 hours)


## Climbing Strategy

### Core Principles
- **Maintain steady power** — do not surge at the base of the climb
- NP 10–15% above flat is acceptable (short segment + descent recovery)
- Long climbs (20min+): 85–95% of FTP
- Short climbs (5min): 100–110% of FTP is acceptable

### Seated (Sitting) vs Standing (Dancing)
| Factor | Seated | Standing |
|---|---|---|
| Efficiency | Higher (lower HR) | Lower (HR 5–10bpm higher) |
| Use Case | Long gradual climbs | Short steep pitches, accelerations |
| Muscles | Quadriceps-dominant | Full body (bodyweight leverage) |
| Recommendation | Default mode (80%) | Gradient 10%+ or rhythm changes (20%) |

### Cadence
- Flat: 85–95rpm (personal preference)
- Climbing: 70–85rpm (low cadence, high torque)
- Steep gradient (10%+): 60–75rpm (gearing limitation)
- Excessively low cadence (<60) → increased knee stress

### Descending Strategy
- **Safety first** — records are made on the climbs
- Brake sufficiently before corner entry
- Outside→apex→outside line (but watch for oncoming traffic)
- Braking: alternate hands (prevent finger fatigue)
- Descents are recovery segments — pedal lightly while lowering heart rate


## Group Riding

### Drafting Effect
- 30–50cm behind the lead rider: **25–40% power savings**
- The second rider also saves 3–5%
- In crosswinds: position slightly offset on the sheltered side

### Rotation
- Lead rider: rotate after 30 seconds to 2 minutes (depending on fitness and group size)
- When rotating: pull off smoothly to the side and drift back
- **No obligation to pull** — if struggling, rotate through quickly at the back

### Safety Hand Signals
- Right/left hand extended behind → obstacle direction
- Hand waving → slowing down
- Pointing → obstacle, pothole
- Voice calls: "Car!", "Front!", "Hole!"

### Four Rivers Traverse Tips
- **Certification centers (인증센터)**: Bicycle passport stamps are mandatory
- **Accommodation**: Book motels/pensions near the cycling path (sell out in peak season)
- **Fueling**: Convenience stores spaced 20–40km apart. Carry extra supplies
- **Safety**: Dedicated cycling paths, but watch for pedestrians and electric kickboards


## Anti-Patterns

- **Chasing a time on your first event** — the goal of the first event is to finish. Records come from the second attempt onward
- **Never riding event distance in training** — experience at least 75–80% of event distance during preparation
- **Racing with untested gear or nutrition** — anything unverified is a risk
- **Blindly following a faster group** — exceeding your own pace leads to late-stage collapse
- **Speed competition on descents** — the most dangerous segment. Safety > speed


## Limitations

1. Weather and wind are unpredictable — always have a Plan B (adjust cutoff times, add fueling stops)
2. Group dynamics are hard to predict — exercise judgment when joining groups that are too fast or too slow
3. For extreme events (brevet 600km+), sleep and mental resilience are the deciding factors — beyond the realm of pure fitness
